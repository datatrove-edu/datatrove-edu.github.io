Data shows that since the 9th century, the date of the full flowering of Kyoto's cherry trees happens about 4 days earlier every 70 years.

<!--more-->

{{< figure src="CherryBlossomsPlot.png" width="800px" caption="" >}}

Article from the BBC: “The cherry blossom season, Japan's traditional sign of spring, has peaked at the earliest date since records began 1,200 years ago, research shows.”  March 2020
